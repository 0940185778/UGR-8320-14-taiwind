place that i visitd

The first place I visited is Lalibela

I have always wanted to visit Lalibela and i did , the holy city of Ethiopia, where the ancient rock-hewn churches stand as a testament to the faith and devotion of the people. I was amazed by the architectural and artistic wonders that were carved out of solid stone more than 800 years ago. I felt a sense of awe and reverence as I walked through the narrow passages and tunnels that connected the 11 churches, each with its own unique style and decoration. I admired the intricate carvings and paintings that adorned the walls and ceilings, depicting scenes from the Bible and the lives of the saints. I joined the pilgrims and worshippers who came from all over the country to pray and sing in the sacred spaces. I was moved by the spiritual atmosphere and the beauty of the place. Lalibela was more than just a tourist site, it was a living and breathing expression of the Ethiopian culture and history

The second place I visited is Dubai

Dubai was an amazing place to visit, and I had a wonderful time there. I was amazed by the beauty and the diversity of the city, which offered me a lot of fun and memorable experiences. I saw the magnificent skyscrapers, such as the Burj Khalifa, the tallest building in the world, and the Burj Al Arab, the only seven-star hotel in the world. I also visited the rich heritage and history of Dubai, such as the Bastakiya Quarter, where I learned about the old wind-tower houses and the Dubai Museum, where I discovered the past of the city. Dubai also had many exciting activities, such as skiing in the Mall of the Emirates, which had an indoor ski slope, or safari in the desert, where I rode a camel, watched the sunset, and enjoyed a traditional dinner. Dubai was a place where I experienced the best of both worlds, and that is why I loved it.
